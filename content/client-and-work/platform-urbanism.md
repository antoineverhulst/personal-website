# Platform Urbanism Research

## Subsidising Network Effects: The Case of Carpooling in France

This research examines the economics of carpooling platforms and the impact of public subsidies on network effects in the French transportation sector.

### Publication

**Handbook of Platform Urbanism**
Published by Vrije Universiteit Brussel
Expected publication: December 2025

### Overview

This academic article explores how subsidies influence network effects in carpooling platforms, with a specific focus on the French market. The research combines platform economics theory with empirical data from the carpooling sector to understand how public policy can effectively promote sustainable mobility solutions.

### Key Areas

- **Platform Economics:** Analysis of two-sided markets and network effects in mobility platforms
- **Public Policy:** Examination of subsidy mechanisms and their effectiveness
- **Transportation Economics:** Impact on commuter behavior and modal shift
- **Data-Driven Insights:** Empirical analysis using real-world platform data

### Methodology

The research leverages extensive data analysis from carpooling platforms, combining econometric methods with platform data to measure the impact of subsidy programs on user adoption and network growth.

### Impact

This research contributes to understanding how public authorities can effectively support the development of sustainable mobility solutions through strategic subsidies, while optimizing public spending and maximizing social benefits.

### Related Work

- Participation in Paris Dauphine University panel on data sharing regimes for transport services
- Contribution to Horizon 2020 'GECKO' project on new mobility governance
